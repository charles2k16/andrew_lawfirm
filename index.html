FAQ

General

What is the difference between legal information and legal advice?

Legal information provides general guidance about the law, procedures, or legal terms. For example, explaining what a contract is, or outlining the steps involved in filing a lawsuit. It is general and not tailored to any specific person.

Legal advice, on the other hand, applies the law to your particular situation. For example, telling you how the law affects your specific contract, or what strategy you should take in a case. Legal advice can only be given by a licensed lawyer after reviewing your circumstances.

How long will my case take?

The timeline of a case depends on several factors—such as the complexity of the issues, the type of court, the availability of evidence and witnesses, and whether the case is resolved through settlement or goes to trial. Some matters may be resolved in a few weeks or months (e.g., uncontested matters), while others—especially complex civil or criminal cases—can take a year or more. At our firm, we strive to move your case forward as efficiently as possible while protecting your rights and interests. During your consultation, we’ll provide a realistic estimate of the timeline based on the specifics of your case.

Do you work on contingency (no win, no fee)?

In certain types of cases—such as personal injury or accident claims—our firm may work on a contingency fee basis, meaning you don’t pay legal fees unless we successfully recover compensation for you. For other types of matters—such as family, criminal defense, or corporate cases—we typically use an hourly rate or fixed-fee structure. During your consultation, we’ll clearly explain all fee options so you know exactly what to expect, with no hidden costs.

Advanced Topics

What is legal advice?

Legal advice is professional guidance given by a qualified lawyer about your specific legal situation. It goes beyond general legal information and applies the law directly to your case, helping you understand your rights, responsibilities, and possible outcomes.

How much will my lawyer cost?

The cost of legal services depends on several factors, including the type of case, its complexity, and how much time and resources are required. In our form, we believe in transparency and will always explain fees before we begin.

How do I choose which lawyer is right for me?

Choosing the right lawyer is one of the most important decisions you can make for your case. The right attorney should not only have the expertise you need but also be someone you can trust and feel comfortable working with.
